# Dark Gilded Elegant Visual System

This system is abstracted from the visual language of reference presentations, reusing only the design patterns, not any specific topic, historical narrative, figure, event, or fixed chapter.

## 1. Overall Temperament

- **Keywords**: dark, elegant, gilded, orderly, ceremonial, professional judgment, moderate information density.
- **Applicable scope**: business reports, research reports, training courseware, solution presentations, project retrospectives, strategic briefings, product introductions, technical solutions.
- **Avoid misuse**: do not write the style as ancient history, cultural history, or any fixed subject; the visuals may be elegant, but the content must be modern and fit the user's topic.

## 2. Canvas and Space

- 16:9 widescreen, about 13.33 × 7.5 inches.
- Safe margins: about 0.65–0.85 inches left and right; top content starts after 1.45 inches; reserve space near the footer line.
- Page structures preferred: center axis, left-right columns, 2×2 cards, three-column cards, top-bottom partition, horizontal timeline, layered matrix.
- Whitespace ratio: the main body area of content pages occupies 65%–78%; cover/ending pages have more whitespace to create a ceremonial feel; information-dense pages must still reserve breathing room for the header and footer.

## 3. Color Rules

Recommended palette:

- Dark base: `#180B06` / `#201008` / `#0F0704`
- Cards: `#2B160B` / `#371E0F`
- Gilded: `#D6A616`, used for titles, core metrics, conclusions, and main lines.
- Vermilion: `#C63A2B`, used for risks, conflicts, key problems, starting points, or warnings.
- Teal: `#359168`, used for growth, opportunities, coordination, ecosystems, and positive results.
- Cream-gold: `#C9B58A` / `#F4E7C8`, used for body text, auxiliary descriptions, and low-emphasis tags.

Execution rules:

- At most 3 accent colors per page; the primary accent color usually occupies 60%, secondary colors 30%, and warning/accents 10%.
- Use only dark colors and low-contrast textures for large background areas; accent colors are used only for lines, tags, dots, card top edges, and KPI numbers.
- Do not cover the whole page with vermilion as a base; do not overuse gilded color, otherwise it weakens the hierarchy.

## 4. Background and Texture

- The background uses dark brown-black as the base, overlaid with low-contrast grids, thin lines, borders, and dark ambient circles.
- Ambient circles must be inside the canvas or only slightly touching the edge, with low opacity, and must not steal attention from the body text.
- Content pages use a top title divider line and a bottom footer line; cover, transition, and ending pages may use L-shaped corner marks at the four corners and an inset border.
- Texture is a "completeness layer", not the main subject; no texture may affect text readability.

## 5. Font Hierarchy

- Cover main title: 44–56pt, may use `KaiTi` or a title font with a calligraphic feel; if it contains long English text/product names, split it into the subtitle.
- Content page title: 18–30pt, short judgment-style titles preferred; avoid writing only "Background / Status / Solution".
- Content page subtitle: 9–12pt, use 2–4 phrases to describe the scope, methodology, subject, or value.
- Body text: 8.5–12pt, prefer `Microsoft YaHei`; split longer paragraphs into 2–5 short sentences.
- Footer and data source: 7.5–8.5pt, use low-contrast cream-gold/brown-gold.

## 6. Title and Body Typography

- Titles should be conclusions or viewpoints, for example "How core capabilities form a closed loop" or "Three categories of metrics reveal growth potential".
- Body text follows "one task per page": each page expresses only one main judgment, supported by data, cases, workflow, or comparison.
- Each card has 1 title + 1 metric/tag + 2–4 short sentences; avoid stacking long paragraphs.
- Text alignment is primarily left-aligned; cover/ending/section pages may be centered; numbers and KPIs may be centered.

## 7. Decorative Elements

- You may use thin lines, dots, short tags, diamonds, L-shaped corner marks, inset borders, and low-opacity dark textures.
- Line widths are usually 0.6–1.2pt; borders should not exceed 1.2pt.
- Dots and short tags are used to indicate pacing, module numbers, time nodes, and card sequence numbers.
- Icons preferentially use editable geometric shapes or simple symbols, not relying on complex SVGs.

## 8. Card System

- Card base color: dark semi-transparent or dark brown, low-contrast border, with an accent-color bar on the top/left edge.
- Card corner radius is small and restrained; shadows can be softened, avoiding bright-color projections.
- Card internal hierarchy: number/tag → title → divider → metric/key point → bottom tag.
- 2×2, three-column, and left-right combination cards must all keep consistent inner padding within the same page.

## 9. Image Handling

- Images serve only as background ambiance, case evidence, or scenario description; do not replace editable structure with full-page screenshots.
- Images must have a dark overlay added or be placed in a dark card container to ensure text readability.
- Image cropping preferentially uses 16:9, 4:3, 1:1, or vertical card aspect ratios; borders use gilded/cream-gold thin lines.
- When there are no real images, use abstract geometric illustrations, map/workflow/system placeholders, and data cards to complete the visual hierarchy.

## 10. Page Consistency

- Each page keeps the same background, the same title bar logic, the same footer style, and a unified palette.
- The positions of module tags, page numbers, data sources, and takeaways are fixed, forming a continuous reading rhythm.
- After every 4–6 information-dense pages, it is recommended to insert a transition page, quote page, summary page, or visual display page to adjust the pacing.

## 11. Supplementary Observations from the Reference Image Decks

- **Cover/ending echo**: a dark overlay is layered over a full-bleed image or dark-texture background, with a large centered title, L-shaped corner marks at the four corners, a short tag at the top, and author/page number at the bottom; the ending page reuses the same composition but with more concise copy.
- **Red/gold boundary lines**: many pages use 1–2 thin accent lines at the top or bottom, red lines create tension, gold lines create closure; content page borders are weaker, cover/ending page borders are stronger.
- **Left circular core anchor**: a dark circular seal/badge is used to carry a keyword, stage name, core concept, or key metric; on the right, 4–6 horizontal info bars form a "concept anchor + evidence list".
- **2×2 table of contents and cards**: table-of-contents and KPI pages make heavy use of dark semi-transparent cards, with an accent color on the card top/left, and the interior composed of short tags, titles, descriptions, and page numbers/metrics.
- **Three vertical columns of cards**: suitable for showing three categories of elements, three options, or three kinds of value; each card has a colored edge line or icon circle on top, and a short tag or takeaway at the bottom.
- **Top metric bar**: high-density pages give 3–5 key metrics first, then expand the explanation in the lower half of the page, suitable for executives to scan quickly.
- **Image-text relationship**: pages with images often place the image on the left or as a full-bleed background, with dark cards and metrics on the right/bottom; the image is always unified to the same color tone by a dark overlay.
- **Map/path/timeline**: composed of a horizontal main line, node dots, node labels, and explanatory cards below; key nodes are reinforced with gold or vermilion.
- **Summary page pacing**: summary pages often use a large seal or large keyword as the visual anchor, with multiple conclusion cards on the right and a closing short sentence at the bottom.
